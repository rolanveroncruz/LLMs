**Case Summary: *Heirs of the Late Rev. Fr. Jose O. Aspiras vs. Judge Clifton U. Ganay***

**Nature:** Administrative matter concerning alleged abuse of authority by Judge Clifton U. Ganay in SP Case No. A-1026 (Guardianship of Rev. Fr. Jose O. Aspiras).

**Key Provisions & Allegations:**

*   **Gifts and Favors:** Judge Ganay allegedly solicited and received a P50,000 worth of law books, cellular phones, and prepaid cellular phone cards from the ward's property guardians, without the written consent of the guardians. The source alleges abuse of authority.
*   **Financial Transactions:** Judge Ganay issued orders directing the manager of the Philippine National Bank (PNB) to release funds from the ward's account for the purchase of cellular phones and prepaid cards.
*   **Guardianship Powers:** Judge Ganay asserts he was merely implementing the orders of the guardians. The ruling rejects this, citing his impropriety.

**Statutory & Case Law References:**

*   **New Code of Judicial Conduct for the Philippine Judiciary:** Violations of Sections 13, 14, and 15 of Canon 4 (Propriety) are cited. These sections prohibit judges from soliciting or accepting gifts/favors and prevent court staff from doing the same.
    *   **A.M. No. 03-05-01-SC (New Code of Judicial Conduct).**
*   **Dulay v. Lelina, Jr. (A.M. No. RTJ-99-1516):** Emphasizes the high standard of moral righteousness required of judges and the need to avoid even the appearance of impropriety.
*   **Chan v. Majaducon (A.M. No. RTJ-02-1697):** Reinforces the importance of public faith in the judiciary and the more stringent application of the admonition against impropriety for lower court judges.
*   **Eda√±o v. Asdala (A.M. No. RTJ-06-1974):** Stresses the need for judges to maintain and demonstrate independence, integrity, and impartiality to uphold the public's trust in the judicial system.
*   **Co v. Plata (A.M. No. MTJ-03-1501):** Emphasizes that a judge's private conduct must be free from the appearance of impropriety.
*   **Rule 2.01, Canon 2, Code of Judicial Conduct.**

**Obligations & Liabilities:**

*   Judges have a duty to avoid impropriety and the appearance of impropriety.
*   Judges must maintain independence, integrity, and impartiality.
*   OIC-Clerk of Court was found to be following orders.

**Potential Risks & Contentious Clauses:**

*   **Appearance of Impropriety:** Accepting substantial gifts from parties in a guardianship proceeding creates a strong appearance of impropriety and potential bias.
*   **Judicial Discretion vs. Abuse of Authority:** While a judge has discretion in managing a guardianship case, using the ward's funds for personal benefit or facilitating such use raises serious concerns about abuse of authority.
*   **Explanation of Judge** Judge Ganay's explanations of his actions were deemed unsatisfactory.
*   **Unsigned Complaint** The case originated from an unsigned complaint. This could raise questions about the credibility or motives of the source.

**Ruling:**

*   Judge Ganay was found guilty of violating Sections 13, 14, and 15 of Canon 4 of the New Code of Judicial Conduct.
*   Judge Ganay was fined P20,000.00 with a stern warning.
*   OIC-Clerk of Court Eslao was not penalized.
