**Legal Document Summary: *People v. Rivera, Lorenzo, and Dayrit***

**Case:** G.R. No. 258753, June 26, 2024

**Nature:** Appeal from CA decision affirming RTC conviction of Lourdes Rivera for illegal recruitment in large scale (RA 8042) and three counts of estafa (Art. 315(2)(a) RPC).

**Key Provisions & Holdings:**

*   **Illegal Recruitment in Large Scale:** Affirmed conviction under RA 8042. Establishes all three elements: (1) Rivera lacked POEA license. Cites POEA certifications and testimony of POEA inspector Maranan. (2) Engaged in recruitment activities as defined by Art. 13(b) Labor Code, including promising overseas employment and requiring training. (3) Committed against three or more individuals (Michael, Michelle, and Teresita). *People v. Daud* (734 Phil. 698) is cited regarding the definition of illegal recruitment.
*   **Estafa:** Affirmed conviction under Art. 315(2)(a) RPC. Prosecution proved false pretenses regarding ability to deploy workers, reliance by complainants, and resulting damage. *People v. Dela Cruz* (811 Phil. 745) is cited for the elements of Estafa.
*   **Penalties:**
    *   *Illegal Recruitment:* Life imprisonment and a fine of PHP 1,000,000.00 (maximum penalty due to Rivera being a non-licensee).
    *   *Estafa:* Modified based on RA 10951. Amounts defrauded (PHP 150,000-200,000) fall under *arresto mayor* in its maximum period to *prision correccional* in its minimum period. Indeterminate Sentence Law applied, resulting in a sentence of 2 months and 1 day of *arresto mayor*, as minimum, to 1 year and 1 day of *prision correccional*, as maximum, for each count. Cites *People v. Mandelma* (G.R. No. 238910, July 20, 2022) regarding the application of RA 10951 and the Indeterminate Sentence Law.
    *   *Actual Damages:* Rivera ordered to pay Michael Silva (PHP 150,000), Michelle Silva (PHP 150,000), and Teresita De Silva (PHP 200,000) as actual damages.
    *   *Legal Interest:* 6% per annum on actual damages from the filing of the Information until finality, then 6% per annum from finality until full payment. Cites *Lara's Gifts & Decors, Inc. v. Midtown Industrial Sales, Inc.* (G.R. No. 225433, September 20, 2022) and *People v. Centeno* (G.R. No. 225960, October 13, 2021) on legal interest.

**Obligations:**

*   Rivera is obligated to serve sentences for illegal recruitment and estafa.
*   Rivera is obligated to pay fines for illegal recruitment.
*   Rivera is obligated to pay actual damages to private complainants, with legal interest.

**Liabilities:**

*   Criminal liability for illegal recruitment in large scale and estafa.
*   Civil liability for actual damages.

**Potential Risks & Ambiguities:**

*   **Co-Accused at Large:** Lorenzo and Dayrit remain at large, potentially impacting enforcement and restitution.
*   **Factual Issues:** The Court upheld factual findings of lower courts, limiting appeal scope.
*   **Enforcement:** Collecting fines and damages may present challenges given Rivera's circumstances.

**Relevant Case Law & Statutory References:**

*   Republic Act No. 8042 (Migrant Workers Act)
*   Revised Penal Code, Art. 315(2)(a) (Estafa)
*   Republic Act No. 10951 (Adjusting Penalties)
*   Labor Code, Art. 13(b)
*   People v. Daud, 734 Phil. 698 (2014)
*   People v. Dela Cruz, 811 Phil. 745 (2017)
*   People v. Mandelma, G.R. No. 238910, July 20, 2022
*   Lara's Gifts & Decors, Inc. v. Midtown Industrial Sales, Inc., G.R. No. 225433, September 20, 2022
*   People v. Centeno, G.R. No. 225960, October 13, 2021

**Conclusion:**

The Supreme Court affirmed Rivera's conviction but modified the penalties to comply with RA 10951 and imposed the maximum fine for illegal recruitment. The legal interest calculations were clarified. The document highlights the risks and penalties associated with illegal recruitment and estafa under Philippine law. Further actions should focus on enforcing the judgment and potentially locating the co-accused.
