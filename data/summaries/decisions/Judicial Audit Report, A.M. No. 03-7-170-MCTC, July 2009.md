**Legal Document Summary for Judge Priscilla Hernandez's Administrative Case (A.M. No. 03-7-170-MCTC)**

**Nature:** Disciplinary proceedings against Judge Priscilla Hernandez of the Municipal Circuit Trial Court (MCTC), Jimenez-Sinacaban, Misamis Occidental, stemming from judicial audits and subsequent non-compliance with directives from the Supreme Court (SC) and the Office of the Court Administrator (OCA).  The case also considered her conduct as a member of the Bar.

**Key Provisions & Findings:**

*   **Judicial Audits:** Two audits revealed significant backlogs and irregularities in both the 4th MCTC (Jimenez-Sinacaban) and the 5th MCTC (Clarin-Tudela), where Judge Hernandez served as Acting Presiding Judge. Specific findings included:
    *   Failure to decide cases within the 90-day reglementary period (Article VIII, Section 15 of the Constitution).
    *   Unresolved pending incidents and motions.
    *   Cases without further action or setting for considerable time.
    *   Missing case records (Civil Case No. 771 and Criminal Case Nos. 7881 and 7889).
    *   Improper case management practices (e.g., lack of pagination, unsigned certificates of arraignment).
    *   Violation of Section 5, Rule 11 of the Revised Rules on Criminal Procedure (failure to transmit resolutions and case folders to the Provincial Prosecutor).
    *   Violation of Section 14 of Rule 136 of the Rules of Court.
*   **Non-Compliance with Directives:** Judge Hernandez repeatedly failed to comply with directives from the SC and OCA to explain the audit findings, resolve pending matters, and provide missing case records.
*   **Defense:** Judge Hernandez admitted culpability for delays but attributed them to heavy caseloads due to multiple designations.  This defense was deemed unacceptable as she did not request extensions from the Court to manage her workload.
*   **OCA Recommendations:** The OCA recommended dismissal based on gross incompetence, inefficiency, negligence, and dereliction of duty.  The OCA further noted that the adverse findings in the cases submitted for decision, unresolved motions and preliminary investigations spanned as early as 1996 and 1997 in the 4th and 5th MCTC, respectively.
*   **Revocation of Designation:** Due to the findings and recommendations, Judge Hernandez's designation as Acting Presiding Judge of the 5th MCTC of Clarin-Tudela was revoked through Administrative Order No. 05-2006.

**Obligations:**

*   Duty to decide cases promptly and within the prescribed periods (Article VIII, Section 15 of the Constitution; Canon 3, Rule 3.05 of the New Code of Judicial Conduct).
*   Duty to comply with directives from the SC and OCA.
*   Duty to properly manage case records (Section 14, Rule 136, Rules of Court).
*   Duty to uphold the integrity of the judiciary (Code of Judicial Conduct).

**Liabilities:**

*   **Administrative Liability:** Found liable for gross neglect of judicial duty and gross inefficiency, leading to dismissal from service, forfeiture of benefits, and prejudice to re-employment in government.
*   **Financial Liability:** Fined P20,000 for repeated violations of SC directives and Section 14, Rule 136 of the Rules of Court.  Fined an additional P5,000 for violating Canons 1, 11, 12 and Rule 12.04 of the Code of Professional Responsibility (CPR).
*   **Disciplinary Action as a Lawyer:** The Court explicitly considered the administrative case as disciplinary proceedings against Judge Hernandez as a member of the Bar, pursuant to A.M. No. 02-9-02-SC. The Court further noted that the failure to meet the tenets of the Code of Judicial Conduct is also a violation of Canons 1 and 11 of the Code of Professional Responsibility (CPR):
