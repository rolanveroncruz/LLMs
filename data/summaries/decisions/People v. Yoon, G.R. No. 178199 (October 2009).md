## Legal Document Summary: People v. Yoon Chang Wook

**Document Type:** Supreme Court Decision (G.R. No. 178199, October 5, 2009) affirming the Court of Appeals (CA) decision, which affirmed the Regional Trial Court's (RTC) conviction of Yoon Chang Wook for Rape.

**Key Provisions:**

*   **Charge:** Yoon Chang Wook (Accused-Appellant) was charged with Rape (Crim. Case No. 98-824, Art. 266-A of RA 8353) and Robbery (Crim. Case No. 98-825, Arts. 293-294 of the Revised Penal Code).
*   **RTC Ruling:** Found Yoon Guilty of Rape and Not Guilty of Robbery.  Sentenced to *reclusion perpetua* and ordered to pay damages.
*   **CA Ruling:** Affirmed the RTC's decision *in toto*.
*   **Supreme Court Ruling:** Affirmed the CA Decision with Modification on the amount of exemplary damages.

**Obligations/Liabilities:**

*   Yoon Chang Wook is obligated to serve a sentence of *reclusion perpetua* for the crime of rape.
*   Yoon is liable to pay the private complainant (AAA) Php 50,000 in indemnity and Php 50,000 in moral damages and php 30,000 as exemplary damages.

**Potential Risks/Issues:**

*   **Credibility of the Victim:**  Yoon argues that the victim's testimony was not credible and contained inconsistencies. The Court, however, found these inconsistencies to be minor and not affecting her credibility.
*   **Defense Witness Testimony:** Yoon claims that the testimony of the defense witnesses (Lee Hyeon Sook, Rogelio Loquinario, Abelyn de Vera) create reasonable doubt. Court found their testimonies insufficient to disprove the victim's account of the rape.
*   **Medical Evidence:** Yoon challenges the admissibility and probative value of the medical evidence (exhibits G, G-1 to G-27, and medical reports) used to substantiate the rape charge. The Court found these to be compelling evidence supporting the victim's claims of physical violence.

**Case Law/Statutory References:**

*   **Rape:** Defined and penalized under Art. 266-A par. 1 and Art. 266-B par. 1 of Republic Act (RA) 8353 (Anti-Rape Law of 1997).
*   **Robbery:** defined and penalized under Arts. 293-294 of the Revised Penal Code as amended by Section 9 of RA 7659.
*   **People v. Mateo:** (G.R. Nos. 147678-87, July 7, 2004, 433 SCRA 640.) On intermediate appellate court review of criminal cases.
*   **Credibility of the Witness:** People vs. Custodio, 197 SCRA 538. A few discrepancies or inconsistencies in the testimonies of witnesses referring to minor details, and not in actuality touching upon the basic aspects of the whys and wherefores of the crime, do not impair their credibility.
*   **People vs. Glodo, 433 SCRA 535.** The silence of a victim of rape or her failure to disclose her misfortune to the authorities without loss of material time does not prove that her charge is baseless and fabricated
*   **Exemplary Damages:** People v. Sia, G.R. No. 174059, February 27, 2009.

**Ambiguous/Contentious Clauses:**

*   The award of Php 9,000 and 500,000 Korean Won for medical expenses was deleted by the Supreme Court for lack of substantiation in the records.

**Conclusion:**

The Supreme Court upheld Yoon Chang Wook's conviction for Rape. The court placed significant weight on the victim's credible testimony and the corroborating medical evidence. The Court emphasized the importance of according great respect to the trial court's factual findings and assessment of witness credibility.
