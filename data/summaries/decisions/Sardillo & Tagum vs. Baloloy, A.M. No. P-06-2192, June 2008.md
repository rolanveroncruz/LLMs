**Case Summary: Judge Luisito C. Sardillo, et al. v. Sherwin M. Baloloy, A.M. No. P-06-2192 (June 12, 2008)**

**Nature of the Case:** Administrative complaint against Sherwin M. Baloloy, a process server, for Grave Misconduct.

**Key Provisions/Facts:**

*   **Complaint:** Filed by Judge Sardillo and Atty. Tagum alleging Baloloy failed to serve summons in a Nullity of Marriage case (Civil Case No. C-21018) and misrepresented his ability to do so.
*   **Financial Impropriety:** Baloloy received P3,000 from the plaintiff's counsel for travel expenses to serve the summons in Naga City but failed to serve it promptly and spent the money.
*   **Delay in Service:** Summons issued on November 12, 2004, but served only on December 11, 2004. Baloloy cited a typhoon and attendance at a seminar as reasons for the delay.
*   **Prior Offenses:** This was not Baloloy's first administrative offense. He had been previously fined, suspended, and reprimanded for various misconducts.
*   **Violation of Procedure:** Baloloy failed to follow established procedure for requesting and liquidating travel expenses for service of summons (Rule 41, Sec. 10 (e), A.M. No. 04-2-04-SC).

**Obligations:**

*   Baloloy, as a process server, had a duty to serve summons expeditiously and to follow proper procedure regarding expenses.
*   He had a general obligation to conduct himself with honesty and integrity as a public officer (Section 1, Article XI of the Constitution).

**Liabilities:**

*   The Supreme Court found Baloloy **GUILTY of Grave Misconduct**.

**Penalties:**

*   **Dismissal** from the service with forfeiture of all benefits and privileges (except accrued leave credits).
*   Disqualified from reemployment in any branch or instrumentality of the government.

**Potential Risks/Ambiguities:**

*   **Defense Validity:** Baloloy's defenses regarding the typhoon and seminar were rejected as insufficient justification for the delay, indicating a low threshold for accepting such excuses.
*   **Progressive Discipline:** The decision highlights the importance of progressive discipline. Previous warnings were considered crucial in escalating the penalty to dismissal.
*   **Doctrine of Public Trust:**  The court strongly emphasized the doctrine of public trust, holding Baloloy to a high standard of conduct and accountability.
