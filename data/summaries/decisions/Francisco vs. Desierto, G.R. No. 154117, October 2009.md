## Legal Document Summary: Francisco v. Desierto, G.R. No. 154117

**Document:** Resolution of the Office of the Ombudsman dismissing a complaint filed by Ernesto Francisco, Jr. alleging Plunder and violations of RA 3019 and RA 6713 against Joseph Estrada and numerous other individuals related to the Manila-Cavite Toll Expressway (MCTE) Project. The complaint centered on alleged overpricing and irregularities in the government's acquisition of right-of-way properties from AMVEL Land Development Corporation for the C-5 Link Expressway.

**Key Provisions & Obligations:**

*   **Republic Act No. 7080 (Plunder):** Alleged violation of Section 2, specifically concerning the acquisition of ill-gotten wealth through misuse of official power.
*   **Republic Act No. 3019 (Anti-Graft and Corrupt Practices Act):** Alleged violations of Sections 3(a), 3(e), 3(g), 3(h), and 3(j) involving undue injury to the government, unwarranted benefits to private parties, contracts grossly disadvantageous to the government, and interventions for financial interest.
*   **Republic Act No. 6713 (Code of Conduct and Ethical Standards for Public Officials and Employees):** Alleged violation of Sections 7(a) and (d).
*   **Toll Operations Agreement (TOA):** Defines the responsibilities of the government, Public Estates Authority (PEA), and UEM-MARA Philippines Corporation (UMPC) in the design, construction, operation, and maintenance of the MCTE project. Specifically, concerns PEA's advancement of funds for right-of-way acquisition subject to reimbursement.
*   **Memorandum of Agreement (MOA) between PEA and TRB:** Outlines PEA's obligation to pay for the acquired land, and TRB’s obligation to acquire and negotiate the purchase of the properties affected by the right of way.
*   **Administrative Order No. 50:** Prescribes guidelines for the acquisition of private land for public use, including right of way, by offering a purchase price 10% higher than the zonal value.

**Alleged Liabilities & Potential Risks:**

*   **Plunder:** Potential criminal liability for public officials involved in acquiring ill-gotten wealth through misuse of official power (RA 7080).
*   **Graft & Corruption:** Potential criminal and administrative liabilities under RA 3019 for actions causing undue injury to the government, providing unwarranted benefits to private parties, or engaging in activities presenting a conflict of interest.
*   **Financial Loss to the Government:** Alleged overpricing of AMVEL properties resulted in significant financial loss, burdening the government with unnecessary interest payments.
*   **Undervalued Assets:** AMVEL is alleged to have covered former salt factories with trash and other low-grade filling materials; the government was defrauded by not following AO 50 in the C5 Link project which cost the government P828,633,390.00.
*   **Invalidity of Sale:**  There was a question as to whether the 44,699 sq. meters of land sold to the government had a clean title at the time of sale.

**Key Case Law & Statutory References:**

*   **Arias v. Sandiganbayan (180 SCRA 309):** Cited to support the argument that Gregorio Vigilar, as ex-officio chairman of the TRB, was in no position to be legally responsible for the TRB's acquisition of AMVEL's properties.
*   **Fabian v. Desierto (295 SCRA 470):** Discusses the appeal process for decisions from the Office of the Ombudsman and quasi-judicial bodies.
*   **People v. Woolcock, et al (244 SCRA 235):** Cited by private respondents to support the argument that newspaper and magazine articles are "hearsay twice removed and have no evidentiary value whatsoever."
*   **Civil Code Articles 1475, 1471, 1458, 1468, 1469, 1473:** Discusses the requisites for valid price in a contract of sale.
*   **Revised Administrative Code Sec. 38:** Discusses private respondent Vigilar exercising administrative supervision over the TRB.
*   **Gatmaytan v. Court of Appeals (267 SCRA 487):** Defines forum shopping as the institution of two (2) or more actions or proceedings grounded on the same cause.
*   **Art. 8 par. 2, Revised Penal Code:** Defines a conspiracy exists when two or more persons come to an agreement concerning the commission of a felony and decide to commit it.
*   **People v. Quilaton (324 SCRA 670):** Cites the "well-settled rule" that "conspiracy must be proven as clearly as the commission of the offense itself."
*    **Sec. 20 of Rep. Act No. 6770:** Discusses exceptions to the rule where the Office of the Ombudsman may not conduct the necessary investigation of any administrative act or omission complained of.
*   **Universal Motors v. Court of Appeals (205 SCRA 448):** Discusses  questions of law vs questions of fact.
*   **Patalinhug v. CA (229 SCRA 554):** Stated that tax declarations, which petitioner presented as evidence of the alleged overpriced purchase price of the properties, are neither proof of the true market value of properties nor conclusive evidence of their value.
*   **Sesbreño v. Central Board of Assessment Appeals (270 SCRA 360):** States acquisition cost of a property cannot be the sole basis for determining its fair value.
*   **Tirol v. Commission on Audit (337 SCRA 198):** Discussed the role of the  Supreme Court ordinarily does not interfere with the discretion of the Ombudsman to determine whether there exists reasonable ground to believe that a crime has been committed.
*   **Nava v. Commission on Audit (367 SCRA 263, 269-270):** Provides the remedy for an aggrieved party where the findings of the Ombudsman as to the existence of probable cause is tainted with grave abuse of discretion amounting to lack or excess of jurisdiction by filing a petition for certiorari under Rule 65 of the Rules of Court.
*   **Lazatin v. Ombudsman:** This case is key because it states whether "the Ombudsman correctly ruled that there was enough evidence to support a finding of probable cause pertains to a mere error of judgment."
*   **Morong Water District v. Office of the Deputy Ombudsman (328 SCRA 363, 373):** States "[The] Order and the Resolution of the Ombudsman are based on substantial evidence."
*   **Mamburao, Inc. v. Office of the Ombudsman (344 SCRA 805, 817):** Discusses if  the Ombudsman may dismiss a complaint outright for lack of merit, it necessarily follows that it is also within his discretion to determine whether the evidence before him is sufficient to establish probable cause.
*   **Concerned Officials of the Metropolitan Waterworks and Sewerage System [MWSS] v. Vasquez,[87] citing Felipe Ysmael, Jr. & Co., Inc. v. Deputy Executive Secretary:** Held that  government agencies or bodies dealing with basically technical matters deserve to be disentangled from undue interference from the courts, and so from the Ombudsman as well.

**Ambiguous/Contentious Clauses:**

*   **Valuation of Properties:** The key point of contention is the valuation of the acquired properties. The petitioner alleges overpricing based on the zonal value and AO 50, while the respondents argue that valuation is not solely based on zonal value and that the transactions occurred before AO 50 took effect.
*   **Applicability of Administrative Order No. 50:**  The timing of the perfection of the sale is critical in determining if AO 50 should apply. The Ombudsman's decision hinges on the sale being perfected before AO 50's effectivity.
*   **The Ombudsman's Failure to Conduct a Fact-Finding Investigation and Issue Subpoena Duces Tecum:** The complainant contends that the Ombudsman was supposed to conduct a fact-finding investigation and issue subpoena duces tecum but failed to do so.

**Ombudsman's Ruling:**

*   The Office of the Ombudsman dismissed the complaint for lack of evidence, finding no probable cause for Plunder or violations of RA 3019 or RA 6713.
*   The Ombudsman concluded that the land acquisition process followed the relevant rules and regulations, that the properties were not overpriced based on their classification as commercial lands, and that there was no evidence of corruption or ill-gotten wealth.
*   The Ombudsman held that Administrative Order No. 50 was not applicable as the contract was deemed perfected prior to its enactment.

**Potential Risks & Next Steps:**

*   **Appeal of Dismissal:** The petitioner appealed the Ombudsman's resolution to the Supreme Court, which was the wrong mode of appeal since the proper action is to file a Petition for Certiorari under Rule 65 of the Rules of Court.
*   **Continued Public Scrutiny:** The case will maintain public interest and scrutiny due to allegations of corruption and financial loss involving a major infrastructure project.
