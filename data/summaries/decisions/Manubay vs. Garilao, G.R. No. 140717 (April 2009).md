**Case Summary: Manubay vs. Garilao, G.R. No. 140717**

**Facts:**
Petitioners sought to convert 124 hectares of agricultural land (TCT Nos. 12691, 12357, 12358, 12359 and 12360) in Camarines Sur to residential use. The Municipal Agrarian Reform Officer (MARO) had already issued a Notice of Coverage under the Comprehensive Agrarian Reform Program (CARP) prior to the conversion application. The Department of Agrarian Reform (DAR) denied the conversion application citing DAR-AO No. 12, s. 1994, which prohibits conversion applications on lands with existing Notices of Coverage. The Sangguniang Bayan of Pili had reclassified the land after the notice of coverage.

**Issue:**
Whether the DAR Secretary committed grave abuse of discretion in denying the conversion application based on the existing Notice of Coverage. Whether the petitioners failed to exhaust administrative remedies.

**Ruling:**
The Supreme Court affirmed the Court of Appeals' dismissal of the petition.

**Key Provisions and Obligations:**

*   **DAR-AO No. 12, s. 1994:** Prohibits acceptance of conversion applications on lands already subject to Notices of Coverage under CARP, absent lifting of said notice.
*   **DAR-AO No. 7, s. 1997:** Requires appeal of the DAR Secretary's decision on conversion applications to the Office of the President (OP) or the Court of Appeals.
*   **Comprehensive Agrarian Reform Program (CARP):** Governs land reform and acquisition, with lands under Notice of Coverage subject to compulsory acquisition.

**Liabilities and Potential Risks:**

*   **For Petitioners:** Risk of land being acquired under CARP, inability to develop the land for residential/commercial purposes, potential legal costs.
*   **Non-compliance with Exhaustion of Administrative Remedies:** Failure to appeal to the OP as required by DAR-AO No. 7, s. 1997 prejudiced the petitioners.

**Case Law and Statutory References:**

*   **Rule 65, Rules of Court (Certiorari):** Requirements for a successful petition for certiorari, specifically grave abuse of discretion.
*   **Doctrine of Qualified Political Agency:** Department Secretaries act as alter egos of the President.
*    Pangasinan State University v. Court of Appeals, G.R. No. 162321, 29 July 2007, 526 SCRA 92, 99.

**Ambiguous or Contentious Clauses:**

*   The distinction between a "Notice of Coverage" and a "Notice of Acquisition" was raised, but deemed irrelevant by the Court in light of DAR-AO No. 12, s. 1994 and failure to exhaust administrative remedies.
    *   The date of reclassification in relation to the Notice of Coverage affects any vested right to conversion.

**Legal Analysis for Lawyer:**

This case highlights the importance of adhering to administrative regulations and exhausting administrative remedies. Petitioners' failure to appeal to the OP, as required by DAR-AO No. 7, s. 1997, was a critical factor in the dismissal of their petition. The Court emphasized the limited scope of certiorari and the requirement of demonstrating grave abuse of discretion. The timing of the land reclassification is contentious and should be examined further. The Court upheld the denial of conversion as the Notice of Coverage preceded the reclassification.
