**Legal Document Summary: A.M. No. RTJ-16-2470, Prosecutor Leo T. Cahanap v. Judge Leonor S. Quiñones**

**Nature:** Administrative complaint filed by Prosecutor Cahanap against Judge Quiñones for Gross Ignorance of the Law, Gross Misconduct, and violation of the Code of Judicial Conduct.

**Key Provisions & Allegations:**

*   **Oppression/Misconduct (Sustained):**
    *   Antagonistic behavior towards lawyers (e.g., Atty. Macapado, Atty. Padilla), including scolding them in open court. *See* TSNs cited in the text.
    *   Humiliating treatment of court staff, including shouting and using demeaning language ("bogo ba nimo," "punyeta ka," "buwisit ka").
    *   These actions violate Section 3, Canon 5 and Rule 3.04, Canon 3 of the Code of Judicial Conduct and Section 6, Canon 6 of the New Code of Judicial Conduct.

*   **Habitual Tardiness (Sustained):**
    *   Consistent late arrival to court sessions (9:30-10:00 AM instead of 8:30 AM) despite official hours.
    *   Efforts to conceal tardiness by misrepresenting start times in official records. This violates Supervisory Circular No. 14, Circular No. 13 and Administrative Circular No. 3-99

*   **Dismissed Allegations (Judicial in Nature):**
    *   Orders issued in *People v. Macapato* (release of vehicle), *People v. Tingcang* (provisional dismissal), and *People v. Casido* (dismissal for lack of probable cause) - deemed issues of judicial discretion best addressed through appeal, not administrative action. *See* *Union Bank of the Phils. v. Judge Jorge-Wagan, A.M. OCA IPI No. 07-2716-RTJ* and *Quilo v. Jundarino, 611 Phil. 646, 663 (2009).*
    *   Allegation of asking a private complainant to buy jewelry from her

**Obligations & Liabilities:**

*   Judges are obligated to be punctual (Canons of Judicial Ethics, Supervisory Circulars) and courteous to lawyers, litigants, and staff (Code of Judicial Conduct).
*   Judge Quiñones found guilty of Oppression (Gross Misconduct) and Habitual Tardiness.

**Penalties:**

*   Fine of P40,000 for Oppression.
*   Fine of P20,000 for Habitual Tardiness.
*   Warning against repetition of similar acts.
*   Branch Clerk directed to submit status reports on court working relationship every semester for two years

**Potential Risks & Contentious Clauses:**

*   **Subjectivity in Demeanor:** Charges relating to demeanor are often subjective and fact-dependent, relying heavily on witness testimony and interpretation.
*   **Mitigating Factors:** Previous record, offer of apology, and some instances of compassion may have mitigated the penalties.
*   **Remedial Action:** Continued monitoring of court atmosphere is necessary to prevent future misconduct.

**Case Law & Statutory References:**

*   **Code of Judicial Conduct:** Specifically Canon 3 and Canon 5 which covers impartiality, courtesy and equality.
*   **New Code of Judicial Conduct:** Specifically Canon 6, Section 6 which covers maintain order and decorum
*   **Revised Rules of Court Rule 140, Section 8:** Defines penalties for serious charges like Gross Misconduct.
*   **Supervisory Circular No. 14, Circular No. 13 and Administrative Circular No. 3-99**
*   **Union Bank of the Phils. v. Judge Jorge-Wagan, A.M. OCA IPI No. 07-2716-RTJ**
*   **Quilo v. Jundarino, 611 Phil. 646, 663 (2009).**
*   **Macaraya v. Judge Quiñones, OCA IPI No. 11-3677-RTJ, June 16, 2014 (Unsigned Resolution).**
*   **Yu-Asensi v. Judge Villanueva, 379 Phil. 258, 268 (2000).**
*   **Atty. Mane v. Judge Belen, 579 Phil. 46, 51 (2008).**
*   **Tiongco v. Judge Salao, 528 Phil. 969, 978 (2006)**
*   **Torres V. Judge Villanueva, 387 Phil. 516, 524 (2000).**

**Conclusion:** Judge Quiñones was found administratively liable for Oppression and Habitual Tardiness, resulting in fines and a warning. Allegations of gross ignorance and bias were dismissed as judicial in nature. Close monitoring of the court's environment is recommended to prevent further misconduct.
