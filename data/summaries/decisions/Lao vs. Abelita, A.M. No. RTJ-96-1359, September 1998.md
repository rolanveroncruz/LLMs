## Legal Document Summary: *Benjamin Sia Lao vs. Hon. Filemon C. Abelita III* (A.M. No. RTJ-96-1359)

**Type:** Administrative Case Resolution (Supreme Court En Banc)

**Subject:** Grave misconduct and conduct unbecoming a judge

**Parties:**

*   **Complainant:** Benjamin Sia Lao
*   **Respondent:** Hon. Filemon C. Abelita III, Presiding Judge, RTC Masbate, Branch 44

**Key Provisions/Allegations:**

*   **Forcible Entry:** Judge Abelita allegedly forcibly entered Lao's residence/bodega without consent on April 9, 1995, replacing the lock and depriving Lao of access.
*   **Property Dispute Interference:** During the pendency of a civil case between the parties, the Judge allegedly ordered construction on a disputed property (nursery), cut off the complainant's water supply, and ordered the shooting of complainant's workers (William Lao).
*   **Shooting Incident:** Policronio Agao, allegedly on Judge Abelita's order ("Tirahon na, barilin na"), shot and injured William Lao. Judge Abelita was found to possess an unlicensed firearm (.45 caliber).

**Obligations:** Judicial officers are expected to avoid even the slightest infraction of the law and be the embodiment of justice and the people's sense of justice.

**Liabilities:** Judge Abelita's actions were deemed a violation of the Code of Judicial Conduct, specifically Rule 2.01, Canon 2. The Court found that the shooting was ordered by the judge, and the actions of the respondent were unbecoming a member of the judiciary.

**Potential Risks/Contentious Clauses:**

*   **Factual Disputes:** The case hinged heavily on witness credibility and resolving conflicting testimonies regarding the location of the bodega and the events surrounding the shooting.
*   **"Frame-Up" Defense:** The Judge claimed to be a victim of a frame-up engineered by the Complainant and police officers due to alleged prior prosecution of the father of one police officer, but this was dismissed due to lack of evidence.

**Relevant Case Law & Statutory References:**

*   **Code of Judicial Conduct:** Violated Canon 2, Rule 2.01 (Impropriety).
*   **Presidential Decree No. 1866:** Violation related to illegal possession of firearms.
*   **Evidence Rules:**
    *   Police officers are presumed to have performed their duties in accordance with law (Section 3, Rule 131)
    *   Testimony must be considered as a whole (Rules of construction of testimony)
    *  "*FALSUS IN UNO FALSUS IN OMNIBUS'* is not an ironclad rule in this jurisdiction"
*   **Saburnido vs. Madrono, 209 SCRA 755:** Cited regarding a judge's improper wielding of a firearm.
*   **De la Paz vs. Inutan, 64 SCRA 540:** Cited regarding a judge's conduct reflecting on the judiciary.

**Ambiguous/Contentious Clauses:**

*   The Court had to weigh conflicting evidence and judge the credibility of the witnesses.

**Outcome:**

The Supreme Court found Judge Abelita guilty of conduct unbecoming a member of the judiciary and DISMISSED him from service, with forfeiture of all benefits and prejudice to reemployment in government.
