## Legal Document Summary: Contracts with Artes International, Inc.

**Case:** A.M. No. 12-6-18-SC, August 07, 2018, Re: Contracts with Artes International, Inc. Resolution

**Subject:** Review of contracts between the Supreme Court's Program Management Office (PMO) and Artes International, Inc. (Artes) for events related to the National and Global Forums on Liberty and Prosperity, and Chief Justice Panganiban's retirement.

**Key Provisions & Events:**

*   **Background:** Chief Justice Panganiban’s Judicial Philosophy on safeguarding the liberty and nurturing the prosperity of the people under the rule of law
*   **Contracting Parties:** Ms. Evelyn Toledo-Dumdum (PMO Administrator) and Helen R. Dabao (Artes Executive Producer).
*   **Events Covered:**
    *   National Forum on Liberty and Prosperity (August 2006).
    *   Global Forum on Liberty and Prosperity (October 2006).
    *   Chief Justice Panganiban’s retirement activities (November-December 2006).
*   **Numerous Contracts:** Multiple contracts awarded to Artes for various aspects of each event (e.g., logo design, video coverage, conference bags, technical services, opening/closing ceremonies). Contractual Amounts ranged from 35,224.00 to 998,854.78
*   **Funding:** Largely from the Judicial Reform Support Project (JRSP) funded by a World Bank (WB) loan.
*   **Artes claim for payment:** claim for payment of the total sum of P4,117,936.98
*   **Release, Waiver & Quitclaim:** Artes executed a unilateral Release, Waiver & Quitclaim
*   **Resolution of claim for payment:** CLAIM of Artes International, Inc. for payment EXTINGUISHED

**Obligations:**

*   Artes was contracted to provide event management services, including creative, logistical, and technical support.
*   Ms. Dumdum (on behalf of PMO) had the authority to enter contracts.
*   Contracts were subjected to the guidelines for procurement of goods, works and services

**Liabilities & Potential Risks:**

*   **Procurement Violations:** The Court finds that procurement rules were violated. Failure to adhere to Administrative Circular No. 60-2003 and relevant procurement laws
*   **Splitting of Contracts:** Ms. Dumdum committed splitting of contracts by dividing them into artificial phases in order to make them fall below the threshold for shopping or small value procurement, or evading or circumventing the requirement of public bidding.
*   **Lack of Certificates of Availability of Funds (CAFs):** Failure to obtain CAFs for each contract
*   **Authority exceeded:** amounts authorized exceeded Administrator threshold
*   **Undue Injury to Government:** Public officers who commit "splitting of contracts which exceed procedural purchase limits and competitive bidding" with "imprisonment of not less than six (6) years and one (1) day but not more than fifteen years
*   **COA Observations:** In CY 2006, COA found the splitting of contracts in the procurement of steel filing safe cabinets. In CY 2007, there was an observation regarding the slow utilization of the loan proceeds

**Case Law & Statutory References:**

*   **Republic Act No. 9184 (Government Procurement Reform Act):** While mandating adherence to the general policy of the government that contracts for the procurement of civil works or supply of goods and equipment shall be undertaken only after competitive public bidding, RA 9184 recognizes the country's commitment to abide by its obligations under any treaty or international or executive agreement.
*   **International law principle of pacta sunt servanda:** the Philippine government, particularly the implementing agency, in this case the Supreme Court, is therefore obligated to comply with the terms and conditions of the Loan Agreement.
*   **World Bank Guidelines:** Procurement of goods, works and services required for the Project and to be financed out of the proceeds of the Loan shall be governed by the provisions of Schedule 4 to this Agreement.
*   **SC Administrative Circular No. 60-2003:** To ensure the effective implementation of the Judicial Reform Support Project (JRSP) through the timely procurement of Goods, Works, and Services
*   **Government Auditing Code of the Philippines:** Expenditures of government funds or uses of government property in violation of law or regulations shall be the personal liability of the official or employee found to be directly responsible therefor.
*   **R.A. No. 3019:** Inquire whether any act or omission committed by any party, including Ms. Dumdum, resulted in or caused undue injury to the Government

**Ambiguous or Contentious Clauses/Issues:**

*   **Whether R.A. No. 9184 applies:** Whether the Loan Agreement, an international executive agreement between the Republic of the Philippines and the International Bank for Reconstruction and Development, and the contracts complied with the terms and conditions of the Loan Agreement
*   **Propriety of PMO engaging in procurement activities:**  Whether Ms. Dumdum and the PMO should not have engaged in actual procurement activities
*   **Validity of the Procurement Procedures:** Was Canvassing of all three proponents under these circumstances conducted appropriately?

**Conclusion:**

The Court acknowledged the extinguishment of Artes' claim against the Court, but found possible procurement violations by Ms. Dumdum. As such, Court forwarded this resolution to the OFFICE OF THE OMBUDSMAN and the COMMISSION ON AUDIT as basis for further action against MS. EVELYN DUMDUM.
