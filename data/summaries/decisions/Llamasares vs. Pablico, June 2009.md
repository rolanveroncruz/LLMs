## Summary of A.M. No. P-08-2434-A: Llamasares vs. Pablico

**Nature:** Administrative complaint against Mario M. Pablico, Process Server, RTC Manila, Branch 40, for dishonesty.

**Complainant:** Lyn L. Llamasares, Branch Clerk of Court, RTC Manila, Branch 40.

**Respondent:** Mario M. Pablico, Process Server, RTC Manila, Branch 40.

**Key Provisions and Allegations:**

*   **Falsification of Daily Time Records (DTRs):** Respondent made false entries in his DTRs, with discrepancies compared to the branch clerk's logbook entries.
*   **Unauthorized Absences:** Respondent habitually left the office without logging out or obtaining permission.
*   **Selective Service of Court Processes:** Respondent only served court processes he chose, shifting his responsibilities to other court personnel.
*   **Contumacious Behavior:** Respondent repeatedly ignored court orders to respond to the allegations.

**Obligations:**

*   As a public servant, the Respondent has the obligation to be accountable, responsible, loyal, efficient, and act with integrity, as mandated by Section 1, Article XI of the Constitution (Romero v. Castellano, 440 Phil. 468, 474 (2002)).

**Liabilities:**

*   **Dishonesty:** Found guilty of dishonesty for falsifying DTRs and attending to private affairs during office hours.
*   **Simple Neglect of Duty:** Previously found guilty of simple neglect of duty in A.M. No. P-06-2109.
*   **Accessory Penalties:** Cancellation of Civil Service eligibility, forfeiture of retirement benefits (except accrued leave credits), and disqualification from reemployment in government service (Pagulayan-Torres v. Gomez, A.M. No. P-03-1716, 9 June 2005, 460 SCRA 19, 24-25).

**Case Law & Statutory References:**

*   **Section 1, Article XI of the Constitution:** Public office is a public trust; duty of public officers.
*   **Revised Uniform Rules on Administrative Cases, Rule XVI, Sec. 22(a):** Regarding penalties for dishonesty.
*   **Romero v. Castellano, 440 Phil. 468, 474 (2002):** Cites the standard of conduct for court employees.
*   **Office of the Court Administrator v. Saa, 457 Phil. 25 (2003) and Office of the Court Administrator v. Sirios, 457 Phil. 42 (2003):** Referenced for similar rulings on dishonesty.
*   **Reyes v. Pablico, A.M. No. P-06-2109, 27 November 2006, 508 SCRA 146:** Previous finding of simple neglect of duty.
*   **Pagulayan-Torres v. Gomez, A.M. No. P-03-1716, 9 June 2005, 460 SCRA 19, 24-25:** Regarding the application of accessory penalties.

**Potential Risks:**

*   **Reputational Damage:** The respondent's reputation will be damaged as a consequence of the finding of dishonesty.
*   **Difficulty finding future employment:** Respondent is now permanently barred from reemployment in any government office.

**Outcome:**

*   Respondent found GUILTY of dishonesty. Although already dropped from the rolls, the accessory penalties of dismissal were imposed: cancellation of eligibility, forfeiture of retirement benefits (except accrued leave credits), and disqualification from reemployment in the government service.
