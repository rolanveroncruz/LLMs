## Legal Document Summary: A.M. No. 08-1-30-MCTC

**Case:** Re: Financial Report on the Audit Conducted in the Municipal Circuit Trial Court, Apalit-San Simon, Pampanga

**Nature:** Administrative case against Maria Algabre Chico, Clerk of Court II, for gross dishonesty and malversation of public funds.

**Key Provisions & Findings:**

*   **Shortages:** Significant shortages in JDF, SAJF, LRF, Mediation Fund, Fiduciary Fund, and Sheriff Trust Fund. Initial finding of P132,400 undeposited Fiduciary Fund collection. Final shortage accountabilities reached P155,036.02.
*   **Delayed Remittances (GAAM, Art. 1, Sec. 111):** Repeated delays in remitting collections to the Supreme Court, particularly for JDF and SAJF (Circular No. 13-92 and 5-93).
*   **Unreceipted Collections:** Unreceipted marriage solemnization fees of P11,100.00. This violates Sections 61 and 113, Article VI of the Government Auditing and Accounting Manual.
*   **Improper Handling of Sheriff Trust Fund (AC No. 35-2004, Sec. 10):** Release of transportation allowances to a process server without proper authorization or liquidation.
*   **Fiduciary Fund Mismanagement:** Numerous withdrawals and confiscated cash bonds not reported to the Supreme Court. Undeposited collections reached a maximum of P368,400.00.
*   **Forgery:** Forged Judge Gironella's signature on withdrawal slips and claimants' signatures on acknowledgment receipts for refunded cash bonds (Carmelita Bicomong and Luisa Manarang cases).
*   **Tampered Receipt:** Repeated use of Supreme Court O.R.# 13115334. Specifically, a Xerox was used for the case under Josephine Oida in the amount of 8,000.00.
*   **Money Exhibits:** Jueteng money was deposited with ASCOM Multi-Purpose Cooperative, Inc. with withdrawals made for cash in place of checks.

**Obligations & Liabilities:**

*   **Respondent (Maria Algabre Chico):**
    *   Accountable for all shortages and unremitted funds.
    *   Obligated to ensure proper collection, deposit, and reporting of court funds.
    *   Liable for gross dishonesty, grave misconduct, and violation of court circulars and accounting rules.
*   **Presiding Judge (Teodora R. Gonzales):**
    *   Potentially liable for failing to supervise the Clerk of Court and address initial irregularities.
    *   Obligated to designate a competent and honest replacement for the Clerk of Court.
    *   Obligated to ensure exhibit moneys deposited with ASCOM Multi-Purpose, Inc. are transferred and deposited to the SAJF and JDF accounts.

**Potential Risks & Ambiguous Clauses:**

*   **Judge Gonzales's Liability:** The extent of Judge Gonzales's liability for negligence in supervision.
*   **"All of us":** Refers to employees depositing jueteng money to ASCOM, but individual culpability not determined.
*   **Definition of "Gross Neglect of Duty":** The court defines it by citing SC Circulars.

**Statutory References:**

*   **Presidential Decree No. 1602:** Violation cited concerning jueteng money.
*   **Government Accounting & Auditing Manual (GAAM):** Cited for guidelines on remittances and issuance of receipts. (Art. 1, Sec. 111).
*   **Civil Service Rules (Rule XIV, Section 9):** Defines penalties for dismissal and cancellation of eligibility.
*   **Executive Order No. 292** The penalty of dismissal shall carry with it cancellation of eligibility, forfeiture of leave credits and retirement benefits, and the disqualification for re-employment in the government service.
*   **AC No. 35-2004, Sec. 10** Pertains to Sheriff Trust Fund process server allowances.

**Case Law:**

*   **Re: Report on the Financial Audit Conducted in the MTCC-OCC, Angeles City, 26 June 2006, 492 SCRA 469, 481:** Reiterates that failure to remit funds upon demand constitutes prima facie evidence of personal use.
*   **Re: Report on the Financial Audit Conducted at the Municipal Trial Courts of Bani, Alaminos and Lingayen, in Pangasinan, 462 Phil. 535-543 (2003), 417 SCRA 107, 111.:** Restitution does not negate administrative culpability.
*   **Sollesta v. Mission, A.M. No. P-03-1755, 29 April 2005, 457 SCRA 519, 536:** Highlights the impact of dishonesty on public faith in the courts.
*   **Office of the Court Administrator v. Dureza-Aldevera, A.M. No. P-01-1499, 26 September 2006, 503 SCRA 18, 49:** Underscores the importance of safeguarding funds and proper reporting.

**Outcome:** Maria Algabre Chico found guilty of gross dishonesty and malversation of public funds and dismissed from service with forfeiture of benefits. Criminal charges to be filed against her. Judge Gonzales directed to explain her failure to supervise and address the irregularities.
