**Legal Document Summary (G.R. No. 176842, February 18, 2008)**

**Nature:** Petition for Review on Certiorari under Rule 45, challenging CA decision affirming RTC's dismissal of a Complaint for Reconveyance of Ownership with Damages.

**Parties:**

*   **Petitioners:** Flora Leoncio, Felicia Leoncio, and Clarita Leoncio (representing the interests of the deceased Elpidio Leoncio).
*   **Respondents:** Olympia de Vera and Celso de Vera.

**Subject Matter:** Dispute over ownership of Lot 4659, Cad. 344, Bustos Cadastre, Bulacan.

**Key Provisions/Findings:**

*   **Petitioners' Claim:** Emilia Lopez, petitioners' predecessor, was the sole owner of the lot by virtue of a relinquishment of rights from the heirs of Lorenzo Ramos.
*   **Respondents' Defense:** Emilia Lopez co-owned the lot with her siblings, Macaria and Pascual Lopez. Olympia de Vera acquired the shares of Macaria and Pascual. Respondents presented Tax Declarations and Deeds of Sale.
*   **RTC Ruling:** Dismissed the case, finding petitioners failed to prove Emilia's sole ownership and respondents demonstrated valid claim to 3/5 of the lot. Good faith possessors. Awarded damages to Respondents.
*   **CA Ruling:** Affirmed RTC's dismissal, finding petitioners failed to prove Emilia's sole ownership. Deleted the award of damages. Relinquishment and waiver of rights from Ramos heirs was not offered in evidence. The court noted laches may apply.
*   **SC Ruling:** Denied Petition. Affirmed CA decision.

**Obligations & Liabilities:**

*   **Petitioners:** Obligated to respect Respondents' ownership of 3/5 share of subject lot. Liable for costs.

**Potential Risks:**

*   **Petitioners:** Further litigation could be deemed frivolous, potentially leading to sanctions under Rule 45, Section 6. Res Judicata applies.
*   **Respondents:** While the main issue is resolved, potential future issues could arise regarding the remaining 2/5 share allegedly owned by Emilia Lopez and the validity of the deeds of sale from Macaria and Pascual, especially if any irregularities exist that weren't fully explored in the trial.

**Case Law & Statutory References:**

*   **Rule 45, Rules of Civil Procedure:** Governs petitions for review on certiorari, limiting review to questions of law.

*   **Solidbank Corporation v. Spouses Tan, G.R. No. 167346, April 2, 2007:** Reinforces finality of factual findings of the CA and RTC.

**Ambiguous/Contentious Clauses:**

*   **"Relinquishment and Waiver of Rights"**: Critical document that was *not* offered in evidence, which significantly weakened Petitioners' case.
*   **"Good Faith"**: The finding of good faith possession by Respondents could be challenged if evidence of prior knowledge of a flaw in their title later emerges.
*   Extent of due diligence employed by Olympia De Vera to ensure proper sale of the lot.

**Legal Strategy Considerations:**

*   The Supreme Court deemed the issues raised by the Petitioners as factual questions, which are not within the scope of a Petition for Review under Rule 45. This underscores the importance of properly framing legal arguments to focus on questions of law rather than factual disputes.
