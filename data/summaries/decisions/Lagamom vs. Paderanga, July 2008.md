**Legal Document Summary - *Lagamon v. Paderanga*, A.M. No. RTJ-08-2123 (Formerly OCA-I.P.I. No. 07-2679-RTJ)**

**Subject Matter:** Administrative complaint against Judge Rustico D. Paderanga for undue delay in resolving a criminal case for rape ("People v. Alfredo Simene," Criminal Case No. 1124).

**Key Provisions:**

*   **Constitutional Mandate (Art. VIII, Sec. 15(1))**:  Lower courts must decide cases within three (3) months from submission.
*   **New Code of Judicial Conduct (Canon 6, Sec. 5)**: Judges must perform judicial duties efficiently, fairly, and with reasonable promptness.
*   **Rule 140, Rules of Court (Sec. 9)**: Undue delay in rendering a decision is a less serious charge.

**Alleged Violations:**

*   Failure to terminate trial within 60 days from initial trial (A.O. No. 104-96).
*   Failure to decide the case within 30 days from submission (A.O. No. 104-96).  The decision was promulgated 346 days after submission.

**Respondent's Defenses:**

*   Complainant lacked legal standing. (Rejected as the SC can take cognizance of complaints from citizens).
*   Harassment suit due to ongoing appeal.
*   Heavy caseload, lack of staff (no Clerk of Court, injured stenographer lacking computer skills, PAO lawyer with limited availability).  Respondent admitted not complying with Circular No. 38-98.

**Court's Findings & Ruling:**

*   The court acknowledged the heavy caseload and logistical difficulties, but noted that the judge failed to request an extension of time.
*   This failure constituted gross inefficiency.
*   **Undue delay in rendering a decision is a less serious charge under Section 9, Rule 140 of the Rules of Court.**
*   The Court adopted the OCA's finding of guilt for undue delay.
*   The judge was fined P2,000.00 and sternly warned against repetition.

**Statutory References:**

*   **Article VIII, Section 15 (1) of the 1987 Constitution**
*   **Rule 140 of the Rules of Court (Section 9)**
*   **Administrative Order No. 104-96**
*   **Circular No. 38-98**

**Obligations:**

*   Judges must decide cases within the prescribed periods (3 months from submission).
*   Judges must request extensions if they cannot meet deadlines.
*   Judges must perform judicial duties efficiently and promptly.

**Liabilities:**

*   Failure to decide cases within the prescribed period constitutes gross inefficiency.
*   Penalties range from suspension to fines, depending on the severity and frequency of the infraction.

**Potential Risks & Ambiguities:**

*   While mitigating circumstances (caseload, lack of staff) were considered, they did not excuse the failure to request an extension.
*   The complaint originally alleged a violation of a 60-day trial period, but the Court focused on the delay in decision.  While mentioned, the 60 day requirement was not the main thrust of the Court's ruling.
*   The legal standing of the complainant was questioned but ultimately dismissed, leaving a potential for similar challenges in future administrative cases by parties not directly involved in the underlying case.
*   The relatively light penalty suggests a degree of leniency, but future delays will likely result in more severe sanctions.
