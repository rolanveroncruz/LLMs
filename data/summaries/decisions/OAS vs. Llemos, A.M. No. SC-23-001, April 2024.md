**Legal Document Summary: A.M. No. SC-23-001**

**Nature of Document:** Decision by the Supreme Court regarding an administrative case against Johnny R. Llemos, a painter in the Maintenance Division, Office of Administrative Services (OAS).

**Key Provisions & Findings:**

*   **Charge:** Gross misconduct constituting violations of the Code of Conduct for Court Personnel and use of illegal drugs or substances (methamphetamine).
*   **Evidence:** Positive drug test confirmed by the National Bureau of Investigation (NBI) and Llemos's admission of drug use in his Comment.
*   **Judicial Integrity Board (JIB) Recommendation:** Dismissal from service with forfeiture of benefits and prejudice to re-employment.
*   **Supreme Court Decision:**  The Court finds Llemos GUILTY but modifies the penalty to **suspension from office without salary and other benefits for one year.** A stern warning was also issued, noting a repetition will result in dismissal. Directs Supreme Court Medical and Dental Services to refer Llemos to a suitable drug rehabilitation facility at his own expense.

**Obligations:**

*   Llemos: Must serve the one-year suspension without pay or benefits. Must undergo drug rehabilitation at his own expense.
*   Supreme Court Medical and Dental Services: Must refer Llemos to a suitable drug rehabilitation facility.

**Liabilities:**

*   Llemos: Subject to suspension. Potential dismissal if he repeats the offense.

**Potential Risks & Contentious Clauses:**

*   **Subjectivity in Penalty:** The decision hinges on mitigating circumstances, creating a potential for inconsistent application of penalties in future cases.
*   **Rehabilitation Requirement:** The phrasing "suitable drug rehabilitation facility" is vague and could lead to disputes regarding the suitability and cost of chosen facilities.

**Relevant Case Law & Statutory References:**

*   **Rule 140 of the Rules of Court (as amended by A.M. No. 21-08-09-SC):** Governs disciplinary proceedings against court personnel, including sanctions for serious charges like gross misconduct and drug use.
*   **A.M. No. 23-02-11-SC (Guidelines for the Implementation of a Drug-free Policy in the Philippine Judiciary):**  Allows suspension as a sanction for drug use.
*   **Re: Administrative Charge of Misconduct Relative to the Alleged Use of Prohibited Drug of Castor, 719 Phil. 96 (2013):**  Establishes the importance of maintaining the integrity of the judiciary through the conduct of its personnel.
*   **Court of Appeals v. Rommel P. Labitoria, A.M. No. CA-24-002-P, October 10, 2023:** Demonstrates the court's precedent in imposing a lesser penalty when mitigating circumstances are present in cases of illegal drug use.

**Ambiguous or Contentious Clauses:**

*   The term "suitable drug rehabilitation facility" lacks specific criteria, potentially causing disagreements about what constitutes a suitable program.

**Overall Assessment:**

The Court, while acknowledging Llemos's guilt, opted for leniency based on his admission of guilt, remorse, and family circumstances, aligning with a more rehabilitation-focused approach to drug abuse. The decision emphasizes the Judiciary's commitment to integrity while leaving room for individual consideration and mitigating circumstances.
