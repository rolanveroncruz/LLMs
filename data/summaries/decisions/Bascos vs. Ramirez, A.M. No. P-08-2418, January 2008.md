**Legal Document Summary: *Bascos v. Ramirez*, A.M. No. P-08-2418**

**Nature of Document:** Supreme Court Decision in an administrative matter.

**Parties:**

*   **Complainant:** Ferdinand S. Bascos, manager of *The Valley Times* newspaper.
*   **Respondent:** Atty. Raymundo A. Ramirez, Clerk of Court and Ex-officio Provincial Sheriff, RTC Ilagan, Isabela.

**Key Provisions & Allegations:**

*   **Neglect of Duty & Violation of P.D. No. 1079 & Supreme Court Circular 5-98:** Respondent allegedly failed to follow the executive judge's order regarding raffling of judicial notices, showed partiality toward *Isabela Profile* newspaper, concealed foreclosure cases, and did not include all cases in the raffle for publication as mandated by P.D. 1079.
*   **Extortion:** Complainant alleges respondent demanded commissions in exchange for publication rights.
*   **Insubordination:** Respondent failed to comply with direct orders from the executive judge to provide documents related to extra-judicial foreclosures.

**Obligations & Duties:**

*   **Respondent's Duty:** As Clerk of Court, respondent is obligated to follow court orders and Supreme Court directives, including P.D. No. 1079 and Circular 5-98 regarding publication of judicial notices. This includes conducting a raffle of notices for publication and maintaining accurate records.
*   **Executive Judges Duty**: Executive Judges must comply with P.D. No. 1079, Circular No. 5-98, and En Banc Resolution (A.M. No. 01-01-07-SC) dated October 16, 2001 in distributing judicial and legal notices and other similar announcements for publication to qualified newspapers and periodicals

**Liabilities & Penalties:**

*   Respondent found **GUILTY** of dereliction of duty, gross neglect, insubordination, and violating the Code of Professional Responsibility.
*   **Penalty:** Fine of P20,000.
*   **Warning:** Future similar acts will be dealt with more severely.
*   **Order:** Submit all specified records and documents.
*   **Risk:** Possible criminal charges under Section 6 of P.D. 1079.

**Relevant Case Law & Statutory References:**

*   **Presidential Decree (P.D.) No. 1079:** *Revising and Consolidating All Laws and Decrees Regulating Publication of Judicial Notices...* Defines rules for publication and penalties for violations.
*   **Supreme Court Circular No. 5-98:** Clarifies requirements for publication and emphasizes compliance with P.D. No. 1079.
*   **En Banc Resolution No. A.M. 01-01-07-SC (October 16, 2001):** Guidelines for accreditation of newspapers for publishing judicial notices.
*   **Act No. 3135:** Pertains to extra-judicial foreclosure of real estate mortgages.
*   **Code of Professional Responsibility:** Violated by respondent's actions.

**Ambiguous or Contentious Clauses/Issues:**

*   **Partiality Claim:** While the court finds the respondent guilty, the allegations of extortion remain unsubstantiated, leaving open the possibility of other motivations for the actions.
*   **Judge Bigornia's Order:** The judge's instruction delegating the raffle process to Deputy Sheriffs after they receive it from him is flagged as a potential violation of P.D. No. 1079, which requires the Executive Judge to personally conduct the raffle.
*   **Defense:** The Respondents defense centered around business rivarly and that other members of the court act with similar character.

**Potential Risks:**

*   **Criminal Prosecution:** Respondent faces potential criminal charges under P.D. 1079.
*   **Reputational Damage:** The finding of guilt damages the respondent's professional reputation.
*   **Disciplinary Action:** Further violations could lead to more severe disciplinary action, including suspension or disbarment.
