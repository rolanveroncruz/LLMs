**Case Summary: *Salvador v. Limsiaco, Jr. and Negroprado* (A.M. No. MTJ-08-1695)**

**Nature of the Case:** Administrative complaint against Judge Manuel Q. Limsiaco, Jr. (4th MCTC) for obstruction of justice, undue delay in rendering a decision, gross inefficiency, and against Clerk of Court John O. Negroprado for undue delay in transmitting records and failure to issue a certificate of completeness.

**Key Provisions/Facts:**

*   **Ejectment Case Delay:** Complainant filed an ejectment case in October 2001. Parties were instructed to file position papers by March 15, 2002. Only the complainant complied.  Judge Limsiaco rendered a decision dismissing the case for lack of cause of action only on May 21, 2003 â€“ over a year after the deadline for position paper submission.
*   **Loss of Position Paper Allegation:** Complainant alleged the Judge lost the original copy of his position paper. The Judge denied receiving it.
*   **Appeal and Record Transmittal Delay:** Complainant filed a notice of appeal.  He alleged Clerk Negroprado delayed transmitting the complete records to the RTC.  Negroprado admitted to failing to issue a certificate of completeness, claiming transmittal by June 16, 2003, which the complainant refuted with evidence of a later transmittal date (July 10, 2003).
*   **OCA Report:** The Office of the Court Administrator (OCA) found merit in the complainant's allegations and recommended sanctions.

**Obligations Violated:**

*   **Judge Limsiaco:** Obligation to render judgment within 30 days of receiving position papers or the deadline for their submission (Rule 70, Rules of Court), and 90 days per the Constitution (Art. VIII, Sec. 15).  Obligation to dispose of the court's business promptly and expeditiously and decide cases within the period fixed by law
*   **Clerk Negroprado:** Obligation to promptly transmit complete records on appeal and certify their completeness.

**Liabilities:**

*   **Judge Limsiaco:** Found GUILTY of undue delay in rendering a decision. Sanctioned with a fine of P20,000 with a warning against future similar infractions. The court classified the delay as a less serious offense under Rule 140, Section 9(1), as amended by A.M. No. 01-8-10-SC.
*   **Clerk Negroprado:** Sternly WARNED to be more circumspect in the discharge of his functions.

**Potential Risks/Ambiguities:**

*   **Position Paper Dispute:** Conflicting accounts regarding the loss or non-receipt of the original position paper introduce an element of factual ambiguity.
*   **Transmittal Date:** The discrepancy in the record transmittal dates (Negroprado's claim vs. the RTC stamp mark) remains a factual dispute relevant to Negroprado's culpability, though he was only warned.
*   **No Obstruction of Justice Finding:** The complaint included "obstruction of justice," but the decision only addressed "undue delay." This suggests either a lack of evidence or the subsumption of the "obstruction" claim under the broader "undue delay" finding.

**Relevant Case Law & Statutory References:**

*   **Rule 70, Section 11, Rules of Court:** 30-day period for judgment after receipt of position papers.
*   **Article VIII, Section 15, 1987 Constitution:** 90-day period for lower courts to decide cases.
*   **Rule 140, Section 9(1) as amended by Adm. Matter No. 01-8-10-SC:** Undue delay classified as a less serious offense with corresponding penalties (suspension or fine).
*   **Cited Cases:**
    *   *Report in the Judicial Audit and Inventory of the Records of Cases in the RTC, Branch 43, Roxas, Mindoro Oriental, A.M. No. 93-9-1249-RTC, 22 September 1994, 236 SCRA 631*
    *   *Mosquero v. Legaspi, A.M. No. RTJ-99-1511, 10 July 2000, 335 SCRA 326*
    *   *Tiongco v. Judge Savillo, A.M. No. RTJ-02-1719, March 31, 2006, 486 SCRA 48*
    *   *Office of the Court Administrator v. Eisma, 439 Phil. 601 (2002)*
    *   *Cases Submitted for Decision before Retired Judge Maximo A. Savellano, Jr., RTC-Branch 53, Manila, 386 Phil. 80 (2000)*
    *   *Rules of Court, Rule 140, Section 11 (b)*

**Conclusion:**

The case underscores the judiciary's commitment to timely case resolution. Judge Limsiaco's substantial delay resulted in a significant fine.  While Clerk Negroprado faced a lesser sanction, the warning highlights the importance of diligence in administrative duties related to appeals. The key takeaway is the strict adherence to mandated timelines and the potential for administrative repercussions for non-compliance. Further investigation might be needed to clarify the issues surrounding the loss of position papers.
