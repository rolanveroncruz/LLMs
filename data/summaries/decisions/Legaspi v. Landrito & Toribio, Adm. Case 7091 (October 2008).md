**Subject:** Summary of Legaspi v. Landrito and Toribio, Adm. Case No. 7091

**Facts:**

*   Jofel Legaspi filed a complaint against Attys. Ramon Landrito and Magno Toribio for Grave Misconduct, Grave Dishonesty, Use of Falsified Document and Conduct Unbecoming of a Member of the Bar.
*   The complaint stems from the alleged falsification of a Special Power of Attorney (SPA) by Atty. Landrito, which he then used in a DARAB case. Atty. Toribio is accused of notarizing the SPA despite the absence of the principals.
*   Complainant alleged that two principals could not have executed the SPA in the Philippines on the indicated date because they were residing abroad.

**Key Provisions & Findings:**

*   **Atty. Toribio's Liability:** Found GUILTY of violating the Code of Professional Responsibility and Rules on Notarial Practice. The Supreme Court finds that Atty. Toribio notarized the SPA without the personal appearance of the principals.
*   **Atty. Landrito's Absense of Liability** The Supreme Court dismisses the case against Atty. Ramon Landrito. There is no basis to conclude that he knew of the defect in the notarization of the SPA and introduced the same in evidence with the knowledge of its deficiency.

**Obligations:**

*   Atty. Toribio is suspended from the practice of law for six (6) months.
*   Atty. Toribio's notarial commission (if any) is suspended for six (6) months.
*   All lawyers have an ethical obligation to uphold the Constitution, obey laws, and respect legal processes (Canon 1, CPR).
*   Lawyers owe candor, fairness, and good faith to the court (Canon 10, CPR).
*   Notaries Public must require personal appearances and verify the identity of individuals signing documents, in accordance with Sec. 1 of Public Act No. 2103

**Liabilities:**

*   Atty. Toribio is subject to disciplinary action for violating ethical rules and notarial practice requirements.

**Potential Risks & Contentious Clauses:**

*   The resolution hinges on the absence of the principals at the time of notarization, as established by immigration records and affidavits.
*   The court emphasizes the importance of notarization and the need for notaries public to exercise utmost care, citing *Agagon v. Bustamante* (541 SCRA 286, 292) and *Dela Cruz v. Zabala* (442 SCRA 407, 412).
*   Mitigating circumstances, such as Atty. Toribio's age, were considered in determining the penalty, indicating a potential area for argument in similar cases. *Follosco v. Atty. Mateo* (466 Phil. 305 (2004) - although with reduced penalty.

**Statutory References:**

*   Code of Professional Responsibility (CPR)
*   Public Act No. 2103, Sec. 1 (Acknowledgment requirements)

**Case Law:**

*   *Agagon v. Bustamante*, A.C. No. 5510, December 20, 2007, 541 SCRA 286
*   *Dela Cruz v. Zabala*, A.C. No. 6294, November 17, 2004, 442 SCRA 407
*   *Gokioco v. Mateo*, A.C. No. 4179, November 11, 2004, 442 SCRA 1
*   *Follosco v. Atty. Mateo*, 466 Phil. 305 (2004)

**Conclusion:**

This case highlights the importance of strict compliance with notarial practice rules. The ruling against Atty. Toribio underscores the potential consequences for failure to ensure the personal appearance of principals during notarization. The dismissal of the case against Atty. Landrito suggests a need to prove the attorney's knowledge of, or participation in, the fraudulent notarization to establish culpability.
