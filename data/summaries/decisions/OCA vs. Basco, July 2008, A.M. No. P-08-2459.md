**Legal Document Summary: OCA vs. Basco (A.M. No. P-08-2459)**

**Nature:** Administrative matter concerning habitual tardiness of Serafin S. Basco, Court Interpreter II, MTCC, Branch 2, Antipolo City.

**Key Provisions & Findings:**

*   **Habitual Tardiness:** Basco incurred tardiness exceeding ten times a month for six consecutive months (Jan-June 2007). This qualifies as "habitual tardiness" under Civil Service Memorandum Circular No. 23, Series of 1998.
*   **Defense:** Basco cited heavy traffic and discouragement from his Clerk of Court to avail flexible working hours.
*   **OCA Recommendation & Court Ruling:** The OCA's recommendation to reprimand Basco was adopted. Basco was found guilty of habitual tardiness. The Court emphasized the stringent standard of conduct required of judiciary employees and the importance of punctuality.

**Obligations & Liabilities:**

*   **Obligation:** Basco, as a court employee, has an obligation to observe prescribed office hours and maintain punctuality, reflecting the public trust inherent in his position.
*   **Liability:** Basco is liable for habitual tardiness under Civil Service Circular No. 19, Series of 1999, specifically Section 52(c)(4), Rule VI. As a first offense, the penalty is a reprimand.

**Potential Risks:**

*   **Future Offenses:** A repetition of the same or similar offense will result in more severe penalties, potentially leading to suspension or dismissal.

**Relevant Case Law & Statutory References:**

*   **Civil Service Memorandum Circular No. 23, Series of 1998:** Defines "habitual tardiness."
*   **Civil Service Circular No. 19, Series of 1999, Rule VI, Section 52(c)(4):**  Specifies penalties for "Frequent unauthorized tardiness (Habitual Tardiness)."
